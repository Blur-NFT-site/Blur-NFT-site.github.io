What Is Blur and How It Changed the NFT Marketplace Landscape

Blur launched in October 2022 with a single clear thesis: the NFT marketplace market was being underserved for professional traders. OpenSea's 2.5% fee, slow interface, and absence of market-making tools created an opening for a trader-first alternative.

Blur's combination of zero marketplace fees, real-time analytics, batch sweeping, collection-wide bidding, and BLUR token incentives for liquidity provision rapidly attracted the traders responsible for the majority of NFT volume. Sweeping: How to Buy Multiple NFTs From a Collection in One Transaction

Sweeping is the act of purchasing multiple NFTs from the same collection in a single transaction — buying up the floor from cheapest to most expensive. Blur's sweeping interface was one of its landmark features at launch, allowing traders to accumulate floor positions efficiently.

How to sweep on Blur

On any collection page, the floor listing view shows items sorted cheapest first. Drag the sweep slider or enter a quantity/max price to select multiple items. Blur batches all selected purchases into a single Ethereum transaction — saving gas compared to individual purchases.

Cross-marketplace sweeping

Blur aggregates listings from multiple marketplaces (OpenSea, LooksRare, X2Y2) in addition to its own. A sweep can execute against the cheapest items available across all integrated platforms — ensuring you always get the absolute floor regardless of where each item is listed.

Collection Bidding: How Blur's Liquidity Model and Bid Pool Work

Collection bidding is Blur's most distinctive innovation — the ability to place a single bid that applies to any item from a collection, not a specific token ID. Sellers who want instant liquidity can accept collection bids at any time — similar to a DEX liquidity pool for NFTs.

How BLUR points are earned

Points accrue through: listing NFTs on Blur at competitive prices, placing collection bids (rewarded most heavily — especially bids close to the floor), sweeping floor items, and generally providing two-sided liquidity. The point system weights collection bidding most heavily because it's the hardest liquidity to provide.

Blend: NFT-Backed Lending Protocol — Borrow ETH Against Your NFTs

Blend (Blur Lending) is Blur's peer-to-peer NFT-backed lending protocol, launched in May 2023. It allows NFT holders to borrow ETH using their NFTs as collateral — without selling their assets or requiring a price oracle.

How Blend works for borrowers

Deposit a supported NFT as collateral and receive ETH from a lender at an agreed interest rate. Keep your NFT exposure while accessing liquidity. Repay the loan plus interest within the term to retrieve your NFT. If you don't repay, the lender can trigger a liquidation auction.

NFT collateralETH liquidityKeep exposure

How Blend works for lenders

Earn interest by offering ETH loans against NFT collateral. If the borrower defaults, you receive the NFT at a discount through a liquidation process. Lending on Blend requires careful assessment of NFT collateral quality — floor price volatility directly affects your collateral value.

Blend risk for both sides: NFT-backed lending introduces compounding risk — if a collection's floor crashes while loans are open, borrowers face liquidation of their NFTs and lenders may recover less than the loan value. The 2023 market showed how quickly these dynamics can cascade in thin markets. Only borrow against NFTs you can afford to lose, and only lend what you'd accept to receive as NFT collateral at the current floor.

Best Practices for Trading on Blur

  • Always verify collection contract addresses — Blur lists collections by name, but fake collections with similar names are common in rising markets. Cross-reference the contract address with the project's official announcement or Etherscan.
  • Use the depth-of-book to assess real liquidity — count how many ETH of genuine bids sit below floor vs BLUR-farming bids. A floor supported only by point-farming bids is far weaker than one backed by genuine accumulation demand.
  • Set collection bid ranges carefully — bids too close to floor earn more BLUR points but also carry higher fill risk in a falling market. Bid depth that fills your wallet with NFTs in a down-trend can lock significant capital.
  • For Blend borrowing: maintain a buffer above liquidation — don't borrow the maximum LTV against your NFT. Floor prices move fast in volatile markets; a buffer buys you time to refinance or repay before liquidation.
  • For Blend lending: only lend against collections with proven floor stability — the highest-yielding loans are against the most illiquid collateral. Lend against blue-chip collections with deep bid depth if capital preservation matters.
  • Revoke Blur contract approvals periodically — especially after large trading sessions. Use Revoke.cash to manage open NFT contract approvals.
  • Bookmark blur.io directly — never access Blur via a link in a DM or social post. The platform handles high-value NFT transactions and is a persistent phishing target.

Troubleshooting Blur: Failed Sweeps, Stuck Bids, and Missing NFTs

"My sweep transaction failed"

  • The most common cause is one or more items in your sweep being purchased by another buyer between your transaction submission and on-chain inclusion — NFT marketplaces are highly competitive and front-running is common at popular floors.
  • Check your transaction on Etherscan for the specific revert reason. If the failure was due to a conflict, simply re-sweep with updated selection.
  • Ensure your ETH balance covers the full sweep amount plus gas — insufficient funds for the entire batch will cause the transaction to fail.

"My collection bid was filled but I didn't want that item"

  • Collection bids are executed by sellers against any item in the collection — you cannot control which specific NFT fills your bid. If you want to avoid certain items (low rarity, specific traits), use trait bids instead of collection-wide bids.
  • List the unwanted item immediately if you don't want to hold it — Blur's zero-fee structure makes quick relisting cost-effective.

"An NFT I sold doesn't appear to have transferred"

  • Verify the sale transaction on Etherscan using your wallet address — confirm the NFT transfer event occurred on-chain. If the transaction succeeded, the NFT has transferred regardless of how your wallet displays it.
  • If the sale transaction failed on-chain (rare), the NFT remains in your wallet. Check Blur's transaction history for your address to see the definitive sale status.
Etherscan is ground truth: For any Blur transaction dispute or confusion, verify the on-chain state via Etherscan before contacting support. The Blur UI may occasionally lag behind on-chain state during high-traffic periods.
